## One chat, one brain

There used to be two AI helpers in here with a little switch between them. We took the switch out. Now there is **one chat**, and it figures out the smart thing to do all by itself. No buttons to flip, no "which mode am I in?" head-scratching.

Open the AI tab and just type. Behind the curtain, the chat quietly picks the best helper for you:

| When you are… | What the chat uses | What it's good at |
|---------------|--------------------|-------------------|
| **Signed in to eggz** with AI credits left | The **eggz assistant** (included, no key) | Your bills, invoices, quotes, accounts and receipts — *and* your tabs and workspaces, all in one place |
| **Signed in** but out of credits, **with your own key** | Your **own Anthropic key** as a backup | Keeps the chat working so you're never stuck mid-sentence |
| **Signed out**, with your own key | Your **own Anthropic key** | Chat about tabs, **Organise**, and save-resources |
| **Signed out**, no key | A friendly sign-in screen | Points you to sign in or add a key |

<Note>
The easiest path is to [connect your eggz account](/help/unscrambled-tabs/eggz-account). When you're signed in, the AI is **included** — no API key, no copy-pasting secret codes, no surprise bills. It's covered by your eggz plan (fair use applies).
</Note>

## It knows what you've got open (the helpful way)

Here's the fun part. The chat doesn't just see the one tab you're staring at. It knows about **every open tab in every window** — even the sleepy ones that Chrome has put down for a nap to save memory. By default it only sees the **names and web addresses** (the titles and URLs), so it knows what you've got going on without poking each tab awake.

It also knows the **names** of things in your **saved library** — your workspaces, folders, and saved links. So you can ask things like:

- *"Which of my open tabs are about the kitchen reno?"*
- *"Did I already save a workspace for the tax stuff?"*
- *"Round up all my shopping tabs and tuck them into one group."*

<Tip>
Sleeping tabs stay asleep. The chat sees the name and address of a napping tab, but it won't wake it up just to peek — that would waste the very memory you were trying to save.
</Tip>

When the AI mentions a tab, it names it like **[Tab: My Page Title]** so you can tell exactly which one it means.

## It asks before it reads a page

Names and addresses are one thing — actually reading **what's written on a page** is another. So the chat **asks first**. When it wants to read the contents of a page (or look at one of your records), a little permission card pops up and you pick:

- **Allow once** — read it just this one time.
- **Always allow** — for *this* conversation, stop asking and let it read when it needs to. (New chat = it asks again.)
- **Deny** — nope, hands off.

<Note>
**Nothing on a page leaves your browser until you say yes.** Until you approve, the chat only ever sees names and web addresses — never the actual words on the page. Your permission choices last for the current conversation only.
</Note>

When you do say yes, the extension reads the page **cleanly** — it pulls out the real article or content and skips the menus, ads, and clutter — so the AI gets the good stuff and not the junk.

## Organise your tabs with a nudge

The **Organise** button now lives right in the chat header. Give it a tap and the AI sorts your open tabs into neat, sensible groups — across **all** your windows, not just the one in front of you. You can then drop those groups straight into a **new or existing workspace** so they're saved for later. Tidy tabs, happy brain.

## "Hey, want to capture that bill?"

If you've been browsing supplier or shop websites, the chat may pop up a little suggestion chip — something like *"You've visited supplier.com today — capture any bills?"* Tap it and your question is already typed out for you.

It only does this after you've visited a couple of different sites recently, so it's a gentle nudge, not a nag. Learn more on [Capture bills from any page](/help/unscrambled-tabs/capture-bills).

## When you run low on AI credits

If your eggz plan's included AI gets used up, the chat doesn't just slam the door:

- If you've added **your own Anthropic key**, the chat quietly switches to it as a **backup** so you can keep going. A small banner lets you know, and you can tap **Upgrade** (or just dismiss it).
- If you **don't** have a key, you'll see a friendly **upgrade card**. Bonus: it also lets you **invite a friend** — when they join, **you both get free AI credits**. Sharing is caring (and also free credits).

## Bring your own key (optional)

Prefer to run on your own steam? You can paste an **Anthropic API key** in **Settings**. Think of it like bringing your own snacks to the movies — totally allowed, and then *you* cover that part of the bill.

A few good-to-knows:

- Your key is stored **on your computer** for the extension, and it's used safely in the background — random websites never get to see it.
- If you're signed in to eggz *and* have a key, the included eggz AI goes first and your key waits in the wings as backup.

<Warning>
Treat your API key like your house key: don't share it or paste it into random places. If you think it leaked, swap it out in the [Anthropic console](https://console.anthropic.com/settings/keys).
</Warning>

## Search anything (⌘K)

Need to find a workspace, a tab, or an action in a hurry? Pop open **Quick search** from your keyboard — from *any* tab:

- **Mac:** ⌘K
- **Windows / Linux:** Ctrl+Shift+K

Start typing and it surfaces matches instantly. Search always uses your own saved stuff on your computer — it doesn't need the internet or your account.

## Jump to the AI assistant (⌘J)

Want the chat right now?

- **Mac:** ⌘J
- **Windows / Linux:** Ctrl+Shift+J

## Not into AI? No problem

You can skip the AI entirely and the rest of the extension hums along just fine. **Workspaces**, **Performance modes**, **snapshots**, and **backup** all work with zero AI and zero key. The AI only taps you on the shoulder for AI-specific things.
