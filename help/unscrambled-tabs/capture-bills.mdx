## Catch bills in the wild

Bills are sneaky. They hide in your email, on supplier websites, and inside PDFs you open and forget about. Unscrambled Tabs helps you grab them the moment you see them — no "I'll deal with it later" pile required.

<Note>
This feature needs you to be [signed in to eggz](/help/unscrambled-tabs/eggz-account), since that's where your captured bills go.
</Note>

## The "looks like a bill" banner

When you land on a page that looks like an invoice or a bill — including PDFs opened right in Chrome — a little banner slides in at the top of the side panel and offers to save it for you.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open a bill or invoice">
    Visit a supplier's invoice page, or open a bill PDF in your browser.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Spot the banner">
    A banner pops up offering to **capture** the page. It's polite — if you ignore it, it tidies itself away after a few seconds.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Send it to eggz">
    Click to capture. The extension grabs the **original invoice** — the actual PDF, filename and all pages intact — and sends it straight into your eggz organisation.
  </Step>
</Steps>

The capture uses the **same upload pipeline and credits** as the dashboard, so your bill lands in [Expenses](/help/guides/expenses) and starts being read automatically — supplier, amounts, tax and all. See [Uploading documents](/help/guides/uploading-documents) for what happens after.

<Tip>
Switched tabs and the banner vanished? No drama. It clears itself when you move to a different tab so it never follows you around. Just go back to the bill page and it'll offer again.
</Tip>

## The gentle "capture any bills?" nudge

The [AI assistant](/help/unscrambled-tabs/ai-and-search) also keeps a light touch on this. If you've been hopping around a few different supplier or shop websites, the chat may show a suggestion chip like *"You've visited supplier.com today — capture any bills?"* Tap it and your message is pre-filled, ready to go.

It only shows up after you've visited a couple of sites recently, so it's a friendly heads-up, not a pushy salesperson.

## What gets sent (and what doesn't)

Keeping it honest:

- When the bill is a **PDF** — including one opened in Chrome's built-in viewer — the capture sends the **original PDF file**, filename and all pages intact, so multi-page invoices arrive complete. Only an on-screen **web-page** invoice falls back to a picture of what's visible.
- It sends anything at all **only when you click to capture**.
- The "looks like a bill" check happens **on your computer**. Pages aren't sent anywhere just for being looked at.
- Your everyday browsing is **not** uploaded. Nothing leaves your browser until you choose to capture.

<Warning>
The bill-detector is smart, but not psychic. It may occasionally wave at a page that isn't really a bill — just ignore the banner and it'll disappear. And when a web page is captured as a picture (rather than an original PDF), glance over the screen first so nothing private tags along.
</Warning>

## If your credits run out

Bills are captured with the same **document credits** as the rest of eggz. If your organisation runs out, the capture card shows an upgrade prompt with a link to **Billing**. Top up and you're back in business.
